> *Working draft for faculty review. A **bounded practice** deepens only in student judgment while its scope deliberately stays flat — the single thread of this type, distinct from the nine spirals and three lenses.*

**In one line:** Depth increases only in the student's critical judgment; the SCOPE of permitted AI use stays narrow and constant — explain, discuss, quiz, critique — never "write this for me and I'll submit it."

---

## Character

Structurally different from spirals and lenses by deliberate constraint. The Year 3–4 Agentic AI specialization depends on comprehension built here; if AI use grew more autonomous over the two years, it would become the crutch the foundation must avoid. So what grows is student judgment, not AI's role. What is EXCLUDED — AI generating substantial code the student merely reviews and submits — should be named explicitly in every host syllabus.

## Passes across the two years

| Block | Host context | What's escalating (scope stays constant) |
|---|---|---|
| B1 (Y1 Fall) | CS-101/102 / CS-105 | AI as explainer for unfamiliar code; student verifies the explanation against actual behavior using the same block's asserts/logs. |
| B5 (Y2 Fall) | CS-202 Database Design | AI as a design-alternatives / tradeoff discussion partner; the final choice and justification are the student's own. |
| B6 (Y2 Fall) | CS-205 Collaborative Development | AI-assisted code review as ONE input among several — not a replacement for human review. |
| B8 (Y2 Spring) | CS-210 Data Analysis & Responsible AI | High-stakes: given AI-GENERATED code, critique it for correctness and security. Prerequisite skill for the Year 3–4 specialization, which this precedes but does not duplicate. |

## Connections

- B8 converges with Trustworthy Computing and Correctness & Verification (verifying AI output for safety and correctness).

## Open questions for faculty review

1. Confirm the flat-scope boundary is right — conservative enough to protect comprehension, not so conservative it undersells what the specialization needs students to trust about AI tools.
2. The exclusion (no AI writing substantial submitted code) should be stated in every host course's syllabus.
