AI-Assisted Development

Working draft for faculty review. A bounded practice deepens only in student judgment while its scope deliberately stays flat — the single thread of this type, distinct from the nine spirals and three lenses.

In one line: Depth increases only in the student’s critical judgment; the SCOPE of permitted AI use stays narrow and constant — explain, discuss, quiz, critique — never “write this for me and I’ll submit it.”

Character

Structurally different from spirals and lenses by deliberate constraint. The Year 3–4 Agentic AI specialization depends on comprehension built here; if AI use grew more autonomous over the two years (the natural Lens growth curve), it would become the crutch the foundation must avoid. So what grows is student judgment, not AI’s role. What is EXCLUDED from Years 1–2 — AI generating substantial code the student merely reviews and submits — should be named explicitly, not left implicit.

Passes across the two years

Block Host context What’s new at this pass
B1 (Y1 Fall) CS-101/102 / CS-105 AI as explainer/study aid for reading unfamiliar code. Student verifies the explanation against actual behavior using the same block’s asserts/logs — AI’s claim vs. empirical check.
B5 (Y2 Fall) CS-202 Database Design AI as a design-alternatives / tradeoff discussion partner. Justification and final choice are the student’s own, articulated independently.
B6 (Y2 Fall) CS-205 Collaborative Development AI-assisted code review as ONE input among several (alongside peer review and tests) — not a replacement for human review.
B8 (Y2 Spring) CS-210 Data Analysis & Responsible AI High-stakes: given AI-GENERATED code, critique it for correctness and security. Students evaluate AI output as a subject of scrutiny. Prerequisite skill for the Year 3–4 specialization, which this precedes but does not duplicate.

Connections

  • B8 converges with the Security and Testing/Verification threads (verifying AI output for correctness and safety).

Open questions for faculty review

  1. Confirm the flat-scope boundary is right — conservative enough to protect comprehension, but not so conservative it undersells what the Year 3–4 specialization will need students to trust about AI tools.
  2. The exclusion (no AI writing substantial submitted code) should be stated in every host course’s syllabus, or faculty may default to more delegation than intended.
